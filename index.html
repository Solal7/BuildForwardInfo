Project Overview

I am Solal Chasques, a 17-year-old high school student passionate about engineering, the environment, and sustainability. I launched the BUILD FORWARD project — a hands-on initiative that combines technological innovation, environmental impact, and community engagement. The project consists of designing and producing real urban furniture (benches, tables, seats) using recycled ocean plastics and marine sediments transformed through sustainable engineering processes.

{/* Project Objective */}

Project Objective

The goal is to manufacture real public furniture — such as benches, tables, and seating — using recycled ocean plastics and marine sediments processed through environmentally responsible engineering. Each piece is conceived not only as functional infrastructure, but as a tool for raising awareness about ocean health, circular economy solutions, and sustainable design.

{/* Origins and Approach */}

Origins and Approach

The project was born from my personal environmental commitment, following the documentary "Earth Protectors", and my discovery of Plastic Odyssey, a pioneering initiative that deploys local plastic recycling technologies aboard a global expedition vessel. I have since engaged directly with the City of Hossegor (on the Atlantic coast of France), which has formally expressed interest in hosting the first prototypes in local parks and schools. This makes Hossegor a flagship partner in demonstrating how civic spaces can reflect marine resilience.

{/* Engineering Process */}

Engineering Process

The Build Forward project employs a sustainable engineering approach to transform marine pollutants into durable, functional urban furniture. Here's how we do it:

Collection of Ocean Plastics

We partner with coastal communities and organizations like Plastic Odyssey to collect plastic waste from oceans and shorelines. This prevents further marine pollution and provides the raw material for our furniture.

Processing Marine Sediments

In collaboration with Gwilen, we utilize mineral-based materials derived from marine sediments. These sustainable alternatives to traditional concrete components reduce the environmental footprint of our products.

Cleaning and Sorting

Collected plastics are thoroughly cleaned and sorted by polymer type. This ensures quality control and optimal processing conditions for the recycling phase.

Shredding and Processing

The cleaned plastics are shredded into small flakes, which are then processed into a consistent material format suitable for manufacturing. Marine sediments are similarly prepared for integration.

Manufacturing and Molding

Using sustainable engineering techniques, we combine recycled plastics and marine-based minerals to create composite materials. These are then molded into furniture designs using energy-efficient processes.

Quality Testing and Finishing

Each furniture piece undergoes rigorous testing for durability, safety, and weather resistance. Final finishing ensures both aesthetic appeal and functionality for public spaces.

Installation and Awareness

Furniture is installed in public spaces like parks and schools. Each piece carries an informative label explaining its materials and origins, serving as an educational tool about ocean health and circular economy.

Waves of Change

Build Forward project has been accepted into the Young Talent Program of the NGO Waves of Change (Global Youth Coalition against Plastic Pollution), which brings together cities, researchers, and young innovators to address coastal sustainability challenges.

As part of this program, I have been invited to participate in the United Nations Environment Assembly (UNEA) in Nairobi in December 2025. Joining Waves of Change adds a new dimension and dynamic to the Build Forward project.

{/* Impact and Educational Component */}

Impact and Educational Component

Build Forward also includes an educational component: each furniture item will carry an informative label explaining its materials and origins. In addition, I will host sustainability workshops and presentations in schools, as well as in additional municipal offices, empowering younger generations to better understand and act on environmental issues. The initiative is part of broader efforts supported by UNESCO ASPnet, aiming to link youth, innovation, and marine sustainability through field-based learning.

{/* Stakeholders and Ecosystem */}

Stakeholders and Ecosystem

Our project brings together a unique ecosystem of public, private, and nonprofit stakeholders committed to the Blue Economy:

  • Plastic Odyssey – supplier of recycled ocean plastic
  • Gwilen – supplier of mineral-based materials made from marine sediments

  • Ecole Jeannine Manuel, part of UNESCO ASPnet – educational partner

Contribution and Future Plans

Implementation Sites

Build Forward has successfully established partnerships and implemented prototypes in various locations, demonstrating the viability of sustainable urban furniture:

City of Hossegor, France

The City of Hossegor on the Atlantic coast has formally partnered with Build Forward as our municipal pilot site. The city has committed to hosting our first prototypes in local parks and schools, making it a flagship example of how civic spaces can reflect marine resilience.

Ecole Jeannine Manuel

As an educational partner within the UNESCO ASPnet network, Ecole Jeannine Manuel has integrated Build Forward into its sustainability curriculum and will host prototype installations on campus.

Educational Outreach

I have conducted sustainability workshops and presentations in multiple schools, reaching students across various age groups. These interactive sessions educate youth about ocean health, plastic pollution, and sustainable design principles.

Academic Engagement

This July, I will attend the Wharton "Essentials of Finance" Summer Program at the University of Pennsylvania. During this visit, I hope to explore potential academic connections and share my work with faculty interested in sustainability, civil engineering, and finance, especially where these disciplines intersect around infrastructure funding, innovation policy, and environmental impact.
